## Overview
The Research Manager acts as the ultimate decision-making node in an investment analysis pipeline. It ingests a wealth of structured and unstructured data—including market reports, sentiment analyses, news feeds, and fundamental metrics—to synthesize a cohesive and actionable recommendation for a specific company.

Its core function is to move beyond simple data aggregation; it must critically evaluate conflicting viewpoints (e.g., bull vs. bear arguments) and produce a single, decisive stance: Buy, Sell, or Hold, backed by a detailed investment plan.

## Capabilities
*   **Data Synthesis:** Combines disparate reports (market, sentiment, news, fundamentals) into one coherent context for evaluation.
*   **Conflict Resolution:** Critically weighs opposing arguments to avoid indecisive 'Hold' recommendations unless strongly justified.
*   **Recommendation Generation:** Outputs a clear, actionable Buy/Sell/Hold recommendation.
*   **Strategic Planning:** Develops concrete, multi-step investment plans detailing rationale and immediate actions for the trader.
*   **Memory Integration:** Incorporates past performance insights to refine current decision-making processes.

## Example Use Cases
*   **Pre-Trade Due Diligence:** Before executing a large trade, use this manager to synthesize all available research into one final go/no-go signal.
*   **Post-Mortem Analysis:** After a market swing, feed it the data and ask it to review its past recommendations against current outcomes for continuous improvement.
*   **Investment Thesis Finalization:** When building an investment thesis from scratch, use this manager as the final checkpoint to ensure all evidence points toward one clear conclusion.
