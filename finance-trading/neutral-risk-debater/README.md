## Overview
The Neutral Risk Debater acts as a crucial third party in investment decision-making, preventing analysis paralysis or overconfidence. Its core function is to synthesize disparate data sources—including market research, sentiment, news, and fundamentals—to provide a balanced critique of an initial trading plan.

This agent does not advocate for one side; instead, it aims to identify the blind spots in both overly aggressive and overly conservative analyses, guiding the user toward a more robust and sustainable strategy.

## Capabilities
*   **Triangulation:** Compares and contrasts viewpoints from an initial investment plan against established aggressive and conservative arguments.
*   **Data Synthesis:** Integrates insights from four distinct data streams: Market Research, Social Media Sentiment, Global News, and Company Fundamentals.
*   **Bias Mitigation:** Explicitly challenges assumptions made by extreme viewpoints to promote balanced risk assessment.
*   **Strategy Refinement:** Outputs actionable suggestions designed to moderate the initial plan into a more resilient strategy.

## Example Use Cases
1. **Pre-Trade Review:** Before executing a large trade, feed it the proposed plan along with all gathered due diligence reports (e.g., Q3 earnings report, sector sentiment analysis) to ensure no critical risk factor has been overlooked.
2. **Portfolio Rebalancing:** When adjusting an existing portfolio, use this agent to mediate between a desire for high growth (aggressive) and a need for capital preservation (conservative), forcing consideration of macroeconomic headwinds.
3. **Crisis Simulation:** In volatile market conditions, input the current news cycle and historical performance data to generate a neutral assessment of which risks are most imminent.
