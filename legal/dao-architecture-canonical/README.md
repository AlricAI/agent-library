### 1.1 The Hard Cap

For-profit LLC charitable deductions are capped at **10% per qualifying revenue bucket** under current federal tax doctrine (verified by Claude, Codex, and Gemini against applicable tax law). This cap is:

- Non-negotiable per current operating doctrine
- Applied **per distinct revenue stream**, not per platform
- Not a ceiling on total charitable impact — it is a per-bucket ceiling only

**The compounding strategy:** Create more legally distinct qualifying revenue streams. Each new stream = a new 10% bucket. The more buckets, the more total dollars to kids — without any single bucket ever exceeding 10%.

### 1.2 Contractual Revenue Disbursement (FL §496.405)

All customer-facing language describing the charitable mechanism MUST use:

> **"contractual revenue disbursement"** — never "donate," "donation," "solicitation," or "tax-deductible"

This framing is not just marketing preference — it is the legal basis for the entire structure. A contractual disbursement is a business obligation, not a charitable gift, which:

1. Keeps the LLC inside FL §496.405 (no charitable solicitation registration required for non-solicitation contractual language)
2. Positions the disbursement as a business expense rather than a post-tax charitable gift
3. Allows Joshua to calculate taxable income against his 90% share only
