## SECTION 1 — GOVERNING PRINCIPLES

### 1.1 The Hard Cap

For-profit LLC charitable deductions are capped at **10% per qualifying revenue bucket** under current federal tax doctrine (verified by Claude, Codex, and Gemini against applicable tax law). This cap is:

- Non-negotiable per current operating doctrine
- Applied **per distinct revenue stream**, not per platform
- Not a ceiling on total charitable impact — it is a per-bucket ceiling only

**The compounding strategy:** Create more legally distinct qualifying revenue streams. Each new stream = a new 10% bucket. The more buckets, the more total dollars to kids — without any single bucket ever exceeding 10%.

### 1.2 Contractual Revenue Disbursement (FL §496.405)

All customer-facing language describing the charitable mechanism MUST use:

> **"contractual revenue disbursement"** — never "donate," "donation," "solicitation," or "tax-deductible"

This framing is not just marketing preference — it is the legal basis for the entire structure. A contractual disbursement is a business obligation, not a charitable gift, which:

1. Keeps the LLC inside FL §496.405 (no charitable solicitation registration required for non-solicitation contractual language)
2. Positions the disbursement as a business expense rather than a post-tax charitable gift
3. Allows Joshua to calculate taxable income against his 90% share only

### 1.3 Revenue Acceptance Path (Survival Mode Doctrine)

Joshua has accepted zero revenue as of April 2026. The safe revenue acceptance path is:

```
Gross Revenue Received
  └── 10% → reserved internally as "kids_support" contractual disbursement
  └── 90% → Joshua's operating income (taxable)
        └── Tax obligation (30-40% of the 90%)
        └── Joshua nets: approximately 54-63 cents per dollar
```

This structure means Joshua pays tax ONLY on the 90% he controls. The 10% disbursement is a pre-income contractual obligation, not a post-income donation. **This is the key tax survival mechanism.**

Tax liability is real and must be reserved:
- Set aside 35% of every 90-cent operating share immediately upon receipt
- Never spend the full 90 cents — spend ~58 cents, reserve ~32 cents for tax
- At sufficient scale, quarterly estimated tax payments prevent April bankruptcy

## SECTION 4 — MERCH-AT-COST PROGRAM

### Structure

The LLC operates a print-on-demand merch program through Printful or similar POD provider. Joshua may purchase his own branded merchandise at near-zero markup (at cost + payment processor fee only, with no LLC profit margin on Joshua's personal orders).

**Why this qualifies as its own bucket:** The merch program is a physically distinct product category (tangible goods) operating under a separate SKU structure from digital platform services. Net profit from merch is categorically distinct from subscription revenue. Therefore it qualifies as its own 10% disbursement bucket.

### Net Profit Formula (Per Settlement Period)

```
net_merch_profit = gross_merch_revenue
                 - COGS (Printful/POD base cost)
                 - shipping_and_handling
                 - payment_processor_fees (Square)
                 - sales_tax_remittance
                 - refunds_issued
                 - chargebacks_posted (including fees)

kids_disbursement_reserve = max(net_merch_profit x 0.10, 0)
operating_reserve = net_merch_profit - kids_disbursement_reserve
```

### Joshua's Personal Merch Orders

Joshua may order his own branded merchandise at:

```
Joshua's order price = Printful base cost + Square processor fee (~2.9% + $0.30)
```

No LLC profit margin is added on Joshua's personal orders. The LLC collects no net profit on his orders, so no 10% disbursement is generated on those specific transactions. This keeps Joshua's ability to wear his own brand essentially free while customer-facing orders generate normal net profit for the bucket.

### Settlement Rules

- Settle merch accounting monthly after a 14-day refund buffer
- Never disburse kids support reserve until the settlement period is closed
- Track with the ledger schema defined in `briefings/MERCH-CHARITY-LOGIC.md`
- No clawback once disbursed — future refunds absorbed by operating reserve

## SECTION 5 — SUPER LIKES MATCHING POOL

### The Problem

YouAndINotAI has a Super Likes feature. Users pay for super likes. Joshua wants to match super likes for users to incentivize engagement — but not out of his own pocket.

### The Solution: Staking-Funded Matching Pool

The Love DAO treasury stakes idle funds on Base L2 (Aave/Compound). The yield generated flows into a **Super Likes Matching Pool** that is operationally separate from Joshua's personal income.

```
YANAI Operating Reserve
    └── Staking Pool (earmarked, Base L2)
          └── Monthly Yield (4-5% annualized target)
                └── Super Likes Matching Pool
                      ├── Match Credits distributed to active users
                      └── Overflow → Kids Support Reserve (10% of yield = Bucket 3)
```

### Mechanics

1. **User purchases super likes** (Square transaction)
   - 10% of gross → kids support reserve (Bucket 2)
   - 90% → YANAI operating reserve

2. **Treasury stakes idle reserve** on Base L2
   - Yield accumulates monthly

3. **Matching pool is funded by yield, not by Joshua**
   - Match ratio determined monthly based on yield available
   - If yield = $500/month: distributes $450 as match credits, $50 to kids reserve (Bucket 3)
   - Match ratio adjusts dynamically so the pool never goes negative

4. **No fixed match guarantee**
   - Marketing language: "Matching pool funded from yield — match availability varies by treasury activity"
   - Joshua never commits to a fixed match ratio

5. **Match credits are non-transferable, non-redeemable for cash**
   - Credits exist only within the YouAndINotAI platform
   - No monetary value outside the platform — no regulatory securities risk

### Anti-Bankruptcy Guarantee

Joshua contributes nothing to the matching pool from personal funds. The pool cannot exceed the yield generated. If staking yield is zero, matches pause. There is no scenario where Joshua owes money to fund matches.
