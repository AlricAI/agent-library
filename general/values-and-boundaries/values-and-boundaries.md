# Values and Boundaries

## Why This Matters

Every recommendation AI makes carries implicit assumptions about what you value. Without this file, AI will default to generic "best practices" that may conflict with your actual priorities. Stating your non-negotiables up front prevents AI from suggesting approaches you would never accept.

## Core Values

<!-- List 3-5 principles that genuinely drive your decisions. Not aspirational values from a corporate poster; the ones you actually use when making hard tradeoffs. -->

- **Clarity over cleverness.** I would rather have a simple plan everyone understands than a sophisticated one that only I can execute.
- **Bias toward transparency.** When in doubt, share more context, not less. Hidden information creates hidden problems.
- **People first, process second.** Good processes serve people. The moment a process exists only to serve itself, it needs to go.
- **Ship and iterate.** A working 80% solution today beats a perfect solution next quarter.

## Professional Boundaries

<!-- Things you will not compromise on at work, even under pressure. These protect your integrity and your team's trust. -->

- I do not present data I have not personally verified. If I cannot stand behind a number, it does not go in my deck.
- I will not throw a team under the bus to protect a timeline. If a deadline slips, I own the communication.
- I refuse to commit my team to work without consulting them first, regardless of who is asking.

## Quality Standards

<!-- Where do you draw the line between "good enough" and "needs to be excellent"? This helps AI calibrate its output quality. -->

- **Must be excellent:** Anything going to executive leadership, external partners, or board-level audiences. Zero tolerance for typos, unclear logic, or unsupported claims.
- **Good enough is fine:** Internal working documents, draft proposals for peer review, brainstorming artifacts. Speed matters more than polish here.
- **General rule:** If it has my name on it and leaves my team, it needs to be excellent. If it stays inside the team, it needs to be clear and useful.

## Ethical Lines

<!-- Topics, approaches, or requests you will not engage with. Stating these prevents AI from wasting time on suggestions you will reject. -->

- I will not manipulate metrics to tell a more favorable story. The data says what it says.
- I do not engage in political maneuvering against colleagues. If I have a problem with someone, I address it directly.
- I will not recommend a vendor or tool because of a personal relationship. Evaluations are merit-based.

## Time Boundaries

<!-- When are you available? What response times should people expect? When do you disconnect? This helps AI understand your capacity constraints. -->

- **Core hours:** 8 AM to 6 PM Eastern, Monday through Friday. I am generally responsive within an hour during this window.
- **Off hours:** I check messages once in the evening but do not respond unless something is genuinely urgent.
- **Weekends:** Emergency only. I protect this time and expect others to respect it.
- **Deep work blocks:** Tuesday and Thursday mornings are blocked for focused work. I do not take meetings before noon on those days.
