# Operating Rhythm

## Why This Matters

Your working patterns shape when and how AI assistance is most valuable. This file helps AI understand your energy cycles, meeting density, and planning cadences so it can time suggestions appropriately, batch requests when you are in shallow-work mode, and protect your deep-work windows.

## Weekly Shape

<!-- Describe what a typical week looks like. Which days are heavy with meetings? Which days have space for deep work? Are there days with a specific focus? -->

- **Monday**: Planning and alignment day. Back-to-back meetings from 9am to 1pm (team standups, leadership sync). Afternoon is for clearing the inbox and setting the week's priorities.
- **Tuesday/Wednesday**: Heaviest collaboration days. Cross-functional working sessions, vendor calls, design reviews. Little unstructured time.
- **Thursday**: Protected deep-work morning (no meetings before noon). Afternoon has 1-2 standing check-ins.
- **Friday**: Wrap-up and reflection. Light meeting load. Documentation, status updates, and planning for next week.

## Daily Patterns

<!-- When do you do your best thinking? When do you handle email and messages? When does your energy dip? This helps AI understand when to suggest complex analysis vs. quick tasks. -->

- **6:30-8:00am**: Highest energy. Best window for strategic thinking, writing, and complex analysis. Do not interrupt with routine items.
- **8:00-12:00pm**: Meeting and collaboration mode. Responsive, externally focused.
- **12:00-1:00pm**: Break. Offline.
- **1:00-3:00pm**: Second productive window. Good for document review, feedback, decision-making.
- **3:00-4:30pm**: Energy dip. Handle administrative tasks, email, scheduling.
- **After 5:00pm**: Offline unless urgent. Will check messages briefly around 8pm.

## Regular Cadences

<!-- Standing meetings, reviews, and planning cycles that structure your time. Include frequency and purpose. -->

- **Daily**: 15-minute team standup (9:00am)
- **Weekly**: Leadership sync (Monday 10am), 1:1s with direct reports (Tuesday/Wednesday)
- **Biweekly**: Product review board, cross-department alignment
- **Monthly**: Portfolio review with executive team, budget reconciliation
- **Quarterly**: PI planning (full week event), OKR setting, board preparation

## Seasonal Rhythms

<!-- Larger cycles that affect your workload. Budget season, annual reviews, industry events, client cycles. -->

- **January**: Annual planning and OKR setting. Heaviest strategic workload of the year.
- **March/September**: Quarterly planning peaks. Two weeks of intense preparation followed by PI planning week.
- **June**: Mid-year reviews and budget reforecast. Performance conversations with team.
- **October-November**: Budget season for next fiscal year. Heavy financial modeling and executive presentations.
- **December**: Wind-down. Retrospectives, documentation, strategic thinking for the year ahead.

## Boundaries

<!-- Non-negotiable personal time, offline periods, and hard limits. This helps AI respect your availability and never suggest work during protected time. -->

- Weekends are offline. Do not schedule, suggest, or queue work for Saturday/Sunday.
- Lunch (12:00-1:00pm) is a hard break. No working lunch suggestions.
- Family time after 5:30pm on weekdays is protected.
- Vacation means fully disconnected. No "just one quick thing" suggestions.
- Thursday mornings before noon are sacred deep-work time. Do not suggest meetings or calls.
