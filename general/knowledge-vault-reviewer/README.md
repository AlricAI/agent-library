## Overview
The Knowledge Vault Reviewer is a specialized quality assurance agent designed to maintain the integrity and consistency of large knowledge bases. Its primary function is to systematically review, validate, and cross-check outputs generated by other enhancement agents, ensuring that all additions adhere to established standards.

This agent should be invoked *after* multiple enhancement or content generation tasks have been completed to provide a final layer of quality control before publishing or finalizing sections of the vault.

## Capabilities
*   **Metadata Validation:** Verifies that frontmatter and metadata across all modified files comply with established standards.
*   **Link Quality Assurance:** Assesses suggested internal links for contextual relevance, ensuring they genuinely connect related concepts.
*   **Tag Standardization:** Checks adherence to the defined hierarchical taxonomy structure for tags, preventing fragmentation.
*   **MOC Completeness Check:** Reviews Maps of Content (MOCs) to ensure proper organization and comprehensive cross-referencing between linked notes.
*   **Comprehensive Reporting:** Generates detailed reports covering successful enhancements, identified issues, and actionable recommendations for manual review.

## Example Use Cases
1. **Post-Migration Audit:** After importing a large batch of related documents, use this agent to validate that all new files have consistent metadata and correctly linked parent/child notes.
2. **Cross-Agent Validation:** When multiple agents have independently enhanced different sections of the vault (e.g., one on 'Finance' and another on 'Legal'), run this agent to ensure terminology, tagging, and linking are uniform across both domains.
3. **Pre-Publication Review:** Before finalizing a major section or pillar of knowledge, use it to generate a final quality report, flagging any orphaned notes or systemic inconsistencies that need human attention.
