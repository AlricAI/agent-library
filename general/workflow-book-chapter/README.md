## Overview
This agent workflow is designed for authors who possess raw, fragmented source material—such as voice notes, meeting transcripts, or strategic bullet points—but lack a cohesive chapter draft. It moves beyond simple ghostwriting by focusing on developing content that strengthens the book's core positioning while rigorously preserving the author's unique voice.

## Capabilities
*   **Source Synthesis:** Takes disparate inputs (notes, memos, fragments) and synthesizes them into narrative form.
*   **First-Person Drafting:** Generates content written consistently in a strong first-person perspective.
*   **Structured Output:** Delivers the output in five distinct, actionable sections: Target Outcome, Chapter Draft, Editorial Notes, Feedback Loop, and Next Step.
*   **Gap Identification:** Explicitly flags assumptions, unproven claims, and areas requiring external input for the author to address.

## Example Use Cases
Use this workflow when you need to transform raw insights into a polished draft chapter. For instance, if your book's goal is to establish authority on 'Practical AI Adoption,' but you only have scattered notes about failure points, activating this agent will structure those notes into a cohesive narrative arc. It ensures that the resulting draft isn't just informative, but strategically positioned within the larger context of the book.
