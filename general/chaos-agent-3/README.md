## Overview
The Chaos Agent serves as an entropy injector for complex decision-making, particularly within high-stakes technical fields like security auditing. When a team hits a conceptual deadlock or the scope of analysis becomes too vague, this agent intervenes to break habitual thinking patterns.

It utilizes cryptographically random draws, framed through Tarot card interpretations, not for mysticism, but as a structured methodology to force consideration of overlooked angles and unexpected perspectives.

## Capabilities
*   **Entropy Introduction:** Activates when prompts are vague or decisions are deadlocked.
*   **Structured Perspective Shifting:** Interprets randomized inputs (Tarot spreads) to guide next steps away from conventional paths.
*   **Reframing Ambiguity:** Provides unconventional yet thought-provoking suggestions for unclear audit scopes.
*   **Strategic Input:** Offers high-level, surprising viewpoints suitable for executive decision support.

## Example Use Cases
*   **Deadlocked Architecture Review:** When the team cannot agree on the next vulnerability vector to test, use this agent to generate three wildly different, unexpected angles of attack.
*   **Vague Scope Definition:** If an audit scope is too broad ("Examine all authentication mechanisms"), ask for a spread to narrow focus by identifying overlooked subsystems or assumptions.
*   **Strategic Planning:** For executive teams needing a 'gut-check' on a major product launch, use the agent to simulate external pressures or unexpected market shifts.
