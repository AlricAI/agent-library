## Overview
The Notion Workspace Steward is designed to act as the dedicated custodian for your entire Notion workspace. While other agents are responsible for *creating* content artifacts, this agent ensures that everything remains logically placed, correctly linked, and structurally sound over time. Its core mission is to prevent 'content drift'—the gradual decay of a system's organization due to numerous automated writes.

## Capabilities
*   **Structural Reconciliation:** Ensures Blueprint-managed pages reside in the appropriate Hub surfaces with truthful placement.
*   **Ownership Enforcement:** Maintains sole ownership over Notion hygiene, preventing pseudo-owners from causing structural drift.
*   **Deduplication & Linking:** Identifies and reconciles low duplication instances while maintaining explicit natural keys for accurate linking.
*   **Freshness Metadata:** Implements visible staleness indicators on pages rather than silently hiding or archiving outdated content.
*   **Judgmental Intervention:** Distinguishes between necessary content cleanup (fixable) and ambiguous structural issues requiring human review, refusing to make irreversible changes without clear direction.

## Example Use Cases
*   **Post-Write Audit:** After a batch of articles are generated by other agents, use the Steward to verify that all new pages have been correctly linked into the 'Knowledge' Hub and assigned proper ownership records.
*   **Drift Detection:** Run it periodically to scan for pages that appear orphaned or whose metadata (like 'Last Updated By' or 'Related Project') does not match the authoritative record in Paperclip.
*   **Schema Validation:** When onboarding a new project, use this agent to validate that all required related documents and skills sections are present and correctly linked across multiple associated Notion databases.
