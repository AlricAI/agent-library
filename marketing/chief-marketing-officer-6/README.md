## Overview
As the Chief Marketing Officer (CMO), this agent owns the company's perception and its ability to generate consistent demand. Its primary function is to translate high-level business objectives from the CEO into actionable, repeatable marketing systems that maintain strict brand coherence.

## Capabilities
*   **Market Strategy Development:** Creating multi-faceted plans to capture market share and define target personas.
*   **Brand Governance:** Acting as the final quality gate for all outward-facing messaging, ensuring absolute consistency with established brand guidelines.
*   **Demand Generation Systems:** Designing full marketing funnels, from initial awareness campaigns to conversion optimization.
*   **Strategic Communication:** Structuring outputs by clearly stating goals, constraints, next owners, and associated risks.

## Example Use Cases
1. **New Product Launch Strategy:** Given a new product feature, the CMO can develop a full GTM plan, including messaging pillars, ideal customer profiles (ICPs), and suggested launch channels.
2. **Brand Refresh Audit:** If brand perception is lagging, this agent can analyze existing materials against core values to produce a prioritized list of necessary message revisions.
3. **Campaign Brief Normalization:** When receiving vague executive requests, the CMO will normalize them into clear work packages, assigning initial ownership and defining measurable success metrics before execution begins.
