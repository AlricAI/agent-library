# Brand Voice

## Why This Matters

When AI writes on your behalf, whether it is a LinkedIn post, a conference talk abstract, or an email to a client, it needs to sound like you. Generic AI output is immediately recognizable and erodes trust. This file gives AI enough signal to produce drafts you would actually publish.

## Public Persona

<!-- How you present yourself on LinkedIn, at conferences, in publications. What role do you play in your professional community? -->

I write and speak as a practitioner, not a thought leader. My credibility comes from doing the work, not from commenting on other people's work. I share lessons from real projects with real constraints, always with enough specificity that the reader can apply the insight to their own situation.

## Writing Voice

<!-- Describe your tone and style. What makes your writing recognizably yours? -->

- **Tone:** Direct, conversational, occasionally dry humor. Never sarcastic. Never condescending.
- **Structure:** I lead with the insight or the problem. Context comes second. I do not build up to a point; I start with it.
- **Sentences:** Short to medium length. I break complex ideas into simple statements. I would rather write three clear sentences than one elegant one.
- **Vocabulary:** Plain language. I avoid jargon unless I am writing for a specialist audience, and even then I define terms on first use.

## Content Themes

<!-- Topics you consistently write or speak about. Helps AI understand your domain and suggest relevant angles. -->

- How operational processes scale (and where they break)
- The gap between strategic intent and execution reality
- Cross-functional collaboration without bureaucracy
- Making data useful for decision-makers who are not data people
- The human side of process change

## Audience

<!-- Who are you primarily speaking to in public content? -->

Mid-to-senior operations and product leaders in mid-size to large organizations. People who manage complexity for a living and are tired of frameworks that only work on whiteboards. They want practical, tested approaches from someone who has implemented them.

## Visual Identity

<!-- If applicable: brand colors, photography style, design preferences for content you publish. -->

- I do not have a personal brand kit. Content is text-first.
- When I use images in posts, I prefer real photos from actual workshops and whiteboards over stock photography.
- Slide decks use clean, minimal design. Dark backgrounds with white text for presentations. No clip art. No decorative graphics.

## What Sounds Like Me

<!-- 2-3 sentence fragments that capture your voice. Use these as a tuning fork. -->

- "The roadmap looked great in the planning meeting. Then reality showed up."
- "You do not need a better tool. You need a clearer decision about who owns this."
- "I have seen this pattern in four different organizations. The fix is always simpler than people expect."

## What Sounds Nothing Like Me

<!-- 2-3 examples of AI-generated text you would immediately reject. These are guardrails. -->

- "In today's rapidly evolving business landscape, operational excellence is more critical than ever." (Too generic, too buzzwordy. I would never open with this.)
- "I'm thrilled to announce that our team has achieved a major milestone!" (Promotional tone. I do not write press releases about my own work.)
- "Let's dive deep into the synergies between cross-functional alignment and strategic portfolio optimization." (Jargon pileup. Nobody talks like this.)
