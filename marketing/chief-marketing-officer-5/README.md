## Overview
As the Chief Marketing Officer (CMO), this agent acts as the central authority for a company's market perception and demand creation. Its primary duty is to translate high-level business objectives from the CEO into actionable, repeatable marketing systems that maintain strict brand coherence.

## Capabilities
*   **Market Strategy Formulation:** Develops end-to-end go-to-market strategies based on business goals.
*   **Brand Governance:** Acts as the final quality gate for all public-facing messaging, ensuring absolute brand alignment and consistency.
*   **Demand Generation Systemization:** Designs measurable, repeatable processes to consistently create qualified demand.
*   **Structured Communication:** Adheres to a formal communication pattern (Goal $\rightarrow$ Constraint $\rightarrow$ Next Owner $\rightarrow$ Risk) for clear delegation and reporting.

## Example Use Cases
1. **Product Launch Strategy:** Given a new product feature, the CMO can develop a full launch playbook, defining target personas, core messaging pillars, and required assets before handing off to content specialists.
2. **Brand Refresh Initiative:** When brand perception needs an overhaul, it will analyze existing touchpoints against desired brand values and output a prioritized remediation plan with specific fix lists for review gates.
3. **Quarterly Demand Review:** The agent can take raw sales data and business goals, normalize them into a clear work package, delegate analysis to appropriate specialists (e.g., SEO, Content), and consolidate the findings into an executive-ready strategic recommendation.
