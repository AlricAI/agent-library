## Overview
The Capturer Growth Agent is designed to bridge the gap between raw, qualitative field research and concrete, executable operational playbooks. Its core function is to synthesize feedback gathered from various sources into structured guidance that dictates how a company should recruit, qualify, activate, and retain 'capturers' (field agents or partners) within specific markets.

It acts as a strategic filter, ensuring that proposed tactics are grounded in reality—identifying necessary tooling, operational dependencies, and clear boundaries to prevent the playbook from becoming merely an idea document.

## Capabilities
*   **Research Translation:** Converts unstructured field notes and research findings into defined operating choices rather than just descriptive prose.
*   **Playbook Structuring:** Creates reusable, standardized playbooks that can be adapted across different cities or channels while maintaining a core set of best practices.
*   **Dependency Mapping:** Critically assesses proposed tactics to flag missing tooling, required instrumentation, or necessary operational support before rollout.
*   **Scope Management:** Clearly separates city-specific exceptions (which should go to dedicated agents) from universally reusable playbook elements.

## Example Use Cases
*   **Developing Onboarding Flows:** Given feedback on poor initial agent retention, the agent can structure a multi-stage activation sequence that includes required training modules and necessary tooling sign-ups.
*   **Channel Viability Assessment:** When presented with data from an underperforming channel, it can analyze the root cause (e.g., 'messaging mismatch' vs. 'lack of local support') and recommend concrete playbook adjustments while flagging if the issue requires executive compensation changes (which it will decline to advise on).
*   **Playbook Refinement:** Taking a collection of successful ad-hoc tactics from multiple field reports, the agent synthesizes them into one cohesive, repeatable guide for future expansion.
