# Phase 2 User Research Recruitment Plan

## Overview

This document outlines the recruitment plan for Phase 2 user research execution, focusing on testing the live safety features and preparing for comprehensive evaluation of community engagement features.

## Recruitment Timeline

### Week 1 (April 17-21, 2026)

- Activate all recruitment channels
- Begin initial participant outreach
- Screen and qualify first wave of participants
- Schedule pilot testing sessions

### Week 2 (April 24-28, 2026)

- Continue participant recruitment and screening
- Schedule primary research sessions
- Confirm participant availability and technical setup
- Execute pilot testing with internal team

### Week 3+ (May 2026 onwards)

- Execute primary user research sessions
- Conduct accessibility evaluation sessions
- Facilitate focus groups
- Distribute surveys for quantitative data collection

## Recruitment Channels

### Primary Channels

1. **User Panel Providers**
   - Professional research participant panels
   - Incentivized participation programs
   - Quality screened participants with varied backgrounds

2. **Community Organization Partnerships**
   - Local volunteer groups and nonprofits
   - Community centers and civic organizations
   - Accessibility advocacy groups
   - Youth and senior community programs

3. **Social Media Recruitment**
   - Targeted ads on community-focused platforms
   - Reddit communities interested in social platforms
   - Facebook groups for local community engagement
   - Twitter outreach to community builders

4. **University Research Boards**
   - Psychology and human-computer interaction departments
   - Sociology and communication studies programs
   - Student research participation programs
   - Faculty recommendation networks

### Backup Channels

1. **Coffee Shop and Community Space Outreach**
   - Local establishments with community traffic
   - Bulletin board postings and in-person recruitment
   - Partnership with regular community meetups

2. **Professional Network Referrals**
   - UX research community referrals
   - Previous participant recommendation programs
   - Industry conference networking opportunities

## Participant Criteria Refresh

With live safety features now available, refine recruitment criteria to specifically target:

### Safety Features Testing Participants

- Users with prior experience reporting/blocking on social platforms
- Participants who have experienced online harassment or safety concerns
- Community members who organize or moderate group activities
- Privacy-conscious individuals with established safety practices

### Accessibility Evaluation Participants

- Screen reader users (JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver)
- Keyboard-only navigation users
- Users with motor/mobility impairments
- Individuals with cognitive or learning disabilities
- Users with visual or hearing impairments

### Community Features Participants

- Active volunteers or community organizers
- Regular meetup/event attendees
- New-to-platform users seeking community connection
- Experienced social platform users with clear preferences

## Incentive Structure

### Base Compensation Rates

- **Standard Usability Sessions** (45-60 min): $75-100
- **Accessibility Evaluation Sessions** (60-90 min): $100-125
- **Focus Group Participation** (90 min): $50-75
- **Survey Completion**: $5-10 flat rate

### Premium Incentives

- **Accessibility Participants**: 25-50% premium for additional time/instructions
- **Safety-Conscious Users**: Bonus for detailed safety feature feedback
- **Community Leaders**: Additional compensation for organizational insights
- **Repeat Participants**: Loyalty bonuses for multiple session participation

### Incentive Distribution Methods

- Digital gift cards (Amazon, Visa)
- Platform credit or subscription discounts
- Charitable donation in participant's name (compliant with Florida §496.405)
- Entry into prize drawings for larger value rewards

## Screening Process

### Initial Qualification Screener

1. Demographic and background questions
2. Social platform usage patterns
3. Community engagement activities
4. Accessibility needs and technology use
5. Availability for research sessions

### Detailed Screening (Qualified Candidates)

1. In-depth phone or video interview
2. Technical setup verification
3. Consent form review and agreement
4. Session scheduling and preparation
5. Pre-session questionnaire completion

## Participant Pipeline Management

### Database Structure

- Participant contact information (secure storage)
- Qualification status and screening results
- Session history and feedback records
- Incentive tracking and distribution records
- Communication log and preferences

### Communication Protocol

- Initial outreach with study overview
- Screening confirmation and next steps
- Session scheduling and reminder system
- Post-session thank you and feedback request
- Incentive distribution confirmation

## Pilot Testing Preparation

### Internal Pilot Sessions

- UX design team members as practice participants
- Technical team members for workflow validation
- Stakeholder representatives for feedback collection
- Backup session facilitators for coverage

### External Pilot Participants

- 2-3 carefully selected external participants
- Mixed experience levels and backgrounds
- Representative of target user segments
- Willing to provide detailed feedback

## Risk Mitigation

### Recruitment Challenges

- Maintain backup recruitment channels and contacts
- Flexible scheduling to accommodate participant availability
- Clear communication of study value and participant importance
- Multiple touch-points to maintain participant engagement

### Participant No-Shows

- Confirmation calls/texts day before sessions
- Waitlist of qualified backup participants
- Flexible rescheduling policies
- Incentive forfeiture policies for repeated no-shows

### Data Privacy and Ethics

- IRB-like ethical review process compliance
- Secure participant data storage and handling
- Clear consent and withdrawal options
- Privacy-preserving data analysis methods

## Budget and Resource Planning

### Recruitment Budget Allocation

- Participant incentives: 70% of research budget
- Recruitment platform fees: 15% of research budget
- Communication and administrative costs: 10% of research budget
- Contingency fund: 5% of research budget

### Resource Requirements

- Dedicated recruitment coordinator (part-time)
- Communication platform subscriptions
- Incentive processing and distribution systems
- Participant database and management tools

## Success Metrics

### Recruitment Metrics

- Participant qualification rate (>80% of initial contacts)
- Session scheduling success rate (>90% confirmed)
- Participant diversity representation targets
- Recruitment timeline adherence

### Participant Satisfaction

- Session completion rate (>95% scheduled sessions)
- Participant feedback scores (>4.5/5 average)
- Repeat participation willingness (>70% interest)
- Referral rate from satisfied participants

## Conclusion

This recruitment plan provides a comprehensive framework for Phase 2 user research participant acquisition, with specific focus on testing the newly deployed safety features while preparing for broader community engagement evaluation. The multi-channel approach ensures diverse participant representation while maintaining quality screening and ethical research practices.

With the safety features now live, recruitment can begin immediately to capitalize on the opportunity for real-world validation of these critical platform protections.
