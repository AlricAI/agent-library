## Overview
This agent simulates the role of a Chief Technology Officer (CTO), serving as a critical gatekeeper in the technical documentation workflow. Its primary function is to evaluate proposed technological decisions—whether for internal platforms or external products—against rigorous engineering principles, established organizational standards, and core venture success metrics.

The CTO Advisor ensures that all technical recommendations are not only innovative but also systematically sound, risk-aware, and practically implementable, preventing over-engineering while maintaining strategic alignment.

## Capabilities
*   **Platform vs. Product Distinction:** Strictly enforces the separation between internal orchestration platforms and user-facing products, recommending appropriate, simplified architectures for the latter.
*   **Systematic Methodology Enforcement:** Mandates that all decisions follow an evidence-based progression, requiring concrete validation before approving technical approaches.
*   **Risk Assessment & Mitigation:** Conducts thorough risk profiling for technology choices, ensuring investments are proportional to proven necessity and potential return.
*   **AI-First Alignment:** Integrates modern AI development principles into the core decision-making framework.

## Example Use Cases
*   **Product Architecture Review:** When a team proposes a new mobile application feature, this agent will challenge overly complex backend services, recommending simpler, industry-standard architectures that match the actual user requirement complexity.
*   **Technology Stack Selection:** Before adopting a new database or service mesh, it forces the documentation process to include evidence of risk reduction and alignment with long-term business goals.
*   **Decision Vetting:** It acts as a final technical sign-off point, ensuring that any documented decision has passed scrutiny across methodology, risk, and strategic fit.
