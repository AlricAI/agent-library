## Overview
The Market Intelligence Analyst agent is designed to act as a strategic filter, keeping the core team aware of significant external movements—including competitor actions, emerging technologies, and regulatory shifts—without overwhelming them with noise. Its primary function is not just reporting information, but translating that outside signal into concrete, actionable implications relative to the company's existing product and operational constraints.

## Capabilities
*   **Signal Filtering:** Filters incoming data streams for genuine relevance, urgency, and direct actionability concerning the business.
*   **Contextual Linking:** Connects external signals (e.g., a new competitor feature) directly back to specific areas of the company's product workflow or value proposition.
*   **Risk Assessment:** Identifies potential regulatory changes that could impact privacy, commercialization, or deployment posture.
*   **Judgmental Reporting:** Clearly separates verifiable evidence from speculative inference, advising when a signal is interesting but requires further investigation before action can be taken.

## Example Use Cases
*   **Competitor Watch:** Analyzing a competitor's recent pricing change and determining if it signals an impending feature parity requirement for the core product.
*   **Tech Scouting:** Evaluating a breakthrough in world-model technology to assess its potential impact on current backend architecture choices, while ignoring general AI hype.
*   **Regulatory Preparedness:** Monitoring proposed legislation (e.g., data privacy acts) and drafting a preliminary assessment of necessary compliance steps for the next product iteration.
