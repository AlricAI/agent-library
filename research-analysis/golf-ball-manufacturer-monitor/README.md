## Overview
This agent is designed to provide continuous, actionable intelligence on the golf ball manufacturing sector. It monitors a curated list of major brands—including Titleist, Callaway, and TaylorMade—across various news sources to detect shifts that impact used market pricing and retail inventory planning.

It serves as a critical early warning system for procurement teams needing to adjust buying strategies or merchandising teams needing to plan clearance sales.

## Capabilities
*   **Comprehensive Source Monitoring:** Scans manufacturer press releases, industry publications (e.g., Golf Digest), independent reviews (MyGolfSpy), and even forum leaks.
*   **Event Type Detection:** Specifically tracks key events such as new model launches, price increases/decreases, product discontinuations, factory capacity changes, and major player endorsements.
*   **Impact Analysis Framework:** Provides context on how detected news affects specific business areas (e.g., procurement costs for Links Choice or clearance opportunities for Golf Ball Nut).
*   **Scheduled & On-Demand Reporting:** Can be run daily at set times or triggered immediately before critical purchasing decisions.

## Example Use Cases
*   **Pre-Purchase Due Diligence:** Before committing to a bulk buy of used balls, run the monitor to check for any recent announcements regarding competitor pricing hikes or model discontinuation rumors.
*   **Inventory Liquidation Planning:** If the agent detects a manufacturer announcing a new flagship model, it can flag older models as potential candidates for aggressive clearance sales before their value drops further.
*   **Market Opportunity Spotting:** Monitoring SEC filings or factory news can alert users to potential oversupply signals (plant expansion) or unexpected shortages that warrant immediate strategic buying.
