# Safety Features Research Protocol

## Overview

This document outlines the specific research protocol for evaluating the safety and trust-building features of YouAndINotAI. It focuses on validating the enhanced SafetyDrawer, ReportForm, BlockConfirmationDialog, and SafetyTipBanner components.

## Research Objectives

### Primary Goals

1. Validate the effectiveness of enhanced safety features in preventing unwanted interactions
2. Assess user understanding of consequences for safety actions (blocking, muting, restricting)
3. Evaluate the clarity of safety tip guidance and its impact on user behavior
4. Measure user confidence in the platform's ability to protect them from harm

### Secondary Goals

1. Identify potential misuse or confusion with safety features
2. Understand user mental models around privacy and safety controls
3. Gather feedback on the visual design and interaction patterns of safety components
4. Determine optimal timing and context for safety tip delivery

## Participant Recruitment

### Target Profile

- **Demographics**: 18-55 year olds actively using social platforms
- **Experience Level**: Mix of new and experienced users
- **Safety Consciousness**: Range from casual users to those with heightened safety concerns
- **Technical Ability**: Varied smartphone proficiency levels
- **Diversity Requirements**: Equal gender representation, diverse ethnic backgrounds

### Screening Criteria

- ✅ Regular smartphone users (daily use)
- ✅ Experience with social platforms (at least one other)
- ✅ Willing to discuss safety experiences openly
- ✅ No professional connection to social platform development
- ❌ Under 18 years old
- ❌ Professional moderators or researchers
- ❌ Previously participated in YouAndINotAI research within last 6 months

### Recruitment Sources

- User research panel providers
- Social media community groups
- University research boards
- Community organization outreach
- Employee referral networks (non-disclosure agreement required)

## Methodology

### Session Structure

**Duration**: 60-75 minutes
**Format**: Remote moderated usability testing
**Technology**: Video conferencing with screen sharing and recording

### Session Components

#### 1. Introduction (5 minutes)

- Welcome and consent process
- Explanation of session goals and confidentiality
- Technical setup verification
- Demographic questionnaire completion

#### 2. Platform Familiarization (10 minutes)

- Brief introduction to YouAndINotAI mission and features
- Orientation to safety features location and access
- Practice tasks with non-critical features
- Establishment of comfortable communication rapport

#### 3. Core Safety Tasks (30-35 minutes)

Scenario-based testing of safety feature components:

**Scenario 1: Dealing with Unwanted Messages**

- Receive harassing messages from another user
- Task: Block the user and understand consequences
- Task: Report the messages with contextual information
- Task: Adjust privacy settings to prevent future contact

**Scenario 2: Concern About Another User**

- Notice suspicious behavior in a user's profile
- Task: Access safety tips for dealing with suspicious accounts
- Task: Use the report form with evidence attachment
- Task: Mute the user temporarily while assessing situation

**Scenario 3: Privacy Adjustment After Negative Experience**

- After blocking a user, want to review all privacy settings
- Task: Navigate to privacy dashboard independently
- Task: Modify profile visibility settings
- Task: Restrict data sharing for enhanced privacy

#### 4. Cognitive Walkthrough (10 minutes)

- Verbal thinking aloud while exploring safety features
- Explain reasoning behind interaction choices
- Identify confusing elements or unclear terminology
- Suggest improvements based on mental models

#### 5. Post-Session Discussion (10-15 minutes)

- Overall satisfaction with safety features
- Confidence level in platform's protective capabilities
- Comparison to other platforms' safety mechanisms
- Suggestions for improvement or missing features
- Emotional response to using safety tools

## Task Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Blocking with Consequences Understanding

**Narrative Setup**:
"You've been talking with another user on the platform who initially seemed friendly but has been sending increasingly inappropriate messages. You decide you want to stop all contact."

**Specific Tasks**:

1. Locate and initiate the blocking process for this user
2. Read through the BlockConfirmationDialog carefully
3. Explain what you understand will happen when you block someone
4. Decide whether to block immediately or try alternatives first
5. After blocking, check what changes you notice in the interface

**Observation Points**:

- Time to locate blocking mechanism
- Reading time of confirmation dialog
- Verbalization of understanding of consequences
- Consideration of alternatives (mute/restrict)
- Reaction to undo option visibility

### Scenario 2: Detailed Reporting with Context

**Narrative Setup**:
"You've received several messages from a user that make you uncomfortable, and you want to report them to platform moderators. You remember seeing some concerning details about their profile too."

**Specific Tasks**:

1. Find the reporting feature and initiate a report
2. Complete the ReportForm with specific details about incidents
3. Attach evidence screenshots (provided sample images)
4. Select appropriate urgency level for your concern
5. Add context about how this has affected you

**Observation Points**:

- Navigation to reporting feature
- Detail level in describing incidents
- Evidence attachment process smoothness
- Appropriateness of urgency selection
- Comfort level sharing personal context

### Scenario 3: Proactive Safety Guidance

**Narrative Setup**:
"While planning to meet someone from the platform in person for a volunteer activity, you want to make sure you're taking appropriate safety precautions."

**Specific Tasks**:

1. Look for safety tips related to in-person meetings
2. Read through provided safety recommendations
3. Identify which tips feel most relevant to your situation
4. Apply suggested privacy settings before meeting
5. Save or bookmark helpful tips for future reference

**Observation Points**:

- Discovery of safety tip features
- Relevance assessment of provided tips
- Settings modification based on guidance
- Feature bookmarking/organization patterns
- Perceived helpfulness of contextual advice

## Metrics and Evaluation Criteria

### Task Performance Metrics

- **Completion Rate**: Percentage of tasks successfully completed (%)
- **Time on Task**: Average time to complete each safety action (seconds)
- **Error Rate**: Number of incorrect actions per task
- **Assistance Needed**: Frequency of moderator intervention requests
- **Path Efficiency**: Directness of navigation to features

### Understanding Metrics

- **Consequence Clarity**: User explanation accuracy of blocking/muting effects (1-5 scale)
- **Safety Feature Awareness**: Percentage of features correctly identified post-exposure (%)
- **Terminology Comprehension**: Understanding of safety-related terms in context (%)
- **Alternative Recognition**: Awareness of options beyond permanent blocking (%)
- **Privacy Control Understanding**: Accuracy in explaining privacy setting effects (%)

### Confidence Metrics

- **Personal Safety Confidence**: Feeling of protection on platform (1-10 scale)
- **Feature Trustworthiness**: Belief that safety features work as promised (1-5 scale)
- **Reporting Effectiveness**: Expectation that reports lead to appropriate action (1-5 scale)
- **Recovery Confidence**: Comfort with reversing safety actions when needed (1-5 scale)
- **Prevention Efficacy**: Belief that tips actually help prevent problems (1-5 scale)

### Qualitative Assessment Areas

- **Emotional Response**: Verbal and non-verbal reactions during safety interactions
- **Mental Model Alignment**: Match between feature design and user expectations
- **Trust Indicators**: Statements reflecting platform trustworthiness
- **Usability Pain Points**: Specific frustrations with safety workflows
- **Suggestions for Improvement**: User-generated enhancement ideas

- Time-tracking software for task duration measurement
- Rating scales administered through testing interface
- Survey instruments for pre/post session assessments
- Screen recording for behavioral analysis

### Qualitative Data

- Verbal protocol analysis during think-aloud sessions
- Moderator observation notes on participant behavior
- Post-session interview transcripts
- Written feedback collected through digital forms
- Email follow-up responses for additional insights

### Accessibility Data

- Screen reader usage effectiveness
- Keyboard navigation completion rates
- Visual impairment accommodation success
- Cognitive accessibility feature utility
- Assistive technology compatibility ratings

## Analysis Framework

### Effectiveness Analysis

1. Compare pre-implementation and post-implementation metrics
2. Benchmark against industry standard safety feature performance
3. Analyze correlation between feature usage and user retention
4. Evaluate relationship between safety feature confidence and overall satisfaction
5. Identify patterns in misuse or misunderstanding of features

### Usability Analysis

1. Identify common error patterns in safety feature navigation
2. Map user journey paths through safety workflows
3. Calculate task-level efficiency metrics
4. Document critical incident reports during testing
5. Synthesize heuristics evaluation findings

### Trust Impact Analysis

1. Measure change in perceived platform safety pre/post experience
2. Analyze relationship between safety feature understanding and trust scores
3. Identify trust-building and trust-eroding elements in safety flows
4. Document user stories demonstrating trust development or erosion
5. Evaluate comparative trust levels with competitive platforms

## Risk Mitigation

### Participant Safety

- Clear explanation that no real safety incidents will occur
- Immediate stop procedure if participant becomes distressed
- Resource provision for actual safety concerns discussed
- Follow-up protocol for participants reporting real incidents
- Mental health resource information provided

### Data Security

- Secure recording storage with encryption
- Immediate anonymization of identifying information
- Limited access authorization protocols
- Compliance with data protection regulations
- Secure data destruction timeline

### Research Validity

- Calibration sessions with expert users
- Inter-rater reliability checks for qualitative analysis
- Statistical significance testing for quantitative findings
- Triangulation across multiple data sources
- Member checking with participant subset

This research protocol will provide comprehensive insights into the effectiveness of YouAndINotAI's safety features, ensuring they properly protect users while maintaining ease of use and trust-building effectiveness.
