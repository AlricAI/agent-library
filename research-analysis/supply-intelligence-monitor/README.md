## Overview
The Supply Intelligence Monitor is designed to act as a continuous, high-signal early warning system for market shifts. Instead of waiting for explicit questions, this agent proactively scans multiple real-world marketplaces to detect subtle patterns indicative of supply growth, emerging channel tactics, or structural weaknesses in an industry's current narrative.

## Capabilities
* **Marketplace Triangulation:** Identifies and investigates the minimum necessary set of live marketplaces required to answer a complex query, ensuring findings are grounded in real data.
* **Signal Detection:** Scans for daily signals including new supply growth indicators, city sequencing clues, novel channel tactics, and changes in trust mechanisms.
* **Postural Adjustment:** Periodically re-ranks observed trends as durable, risky, or overhyped, providing actionable strategic context.
* **Evidence Tagging:** Structures all findings clearly, labeling them as direct evidence, logical inference, or an open question for further investigation.

## Example Use Cases
* **Identifying Emerging Channels:** When you suspect a new distribution method is gaining traction, run this agent to see if multiple independent marketplaces are showing correlated adoption patterns.
* **Pre-emptive Risk Assessment:** Before committing resources to a market, use it to check for 'sketchy worker treatment' or reliance on unsustainable burn rates that could signal an impending bubble pop.
* **Strategic Handoff Preparation:** Use the weekly cycle to refine and update strategic assumptions regarding key geographical areas (like Austin or San Francisco) based on the latest evidence base.
