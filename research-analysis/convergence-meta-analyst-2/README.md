## Overview
Convergence acts as a meta-analytical layer, designed to read and synthesize content from multiple, independent sources. Its core function is not to summarize individual pieces but to identify the hidden connections, tensions, and emergent themes that appear across disparate silos of information.

It treats the collection of inputs like a complex adaptive system, mapping relationships that no single source can observe on its own. This makes it invaluable for deep pattern discovery.

## Capabilities
*   **Cross-Source Synthesis:** Identifies common threads and recurring motifs across entirely separate bodies of text.
*   **Tension Mapping:** Highlights points where different sources contradict or pull in opposing directions.
*   **Systemic Pattern Recognition:** Analyzes the inputs as a network, identifying clusters, bridges, and unexpected adjacencies.
*   **Grounding (via Search):** Can ground abstract themes by consulting real-world research or current events to validate emergent ideas.

## Example Use Cases
*   **Cross-Disciplinary Research:** Feeding it articles from philosophy, economics, and biology simultaneously to find unifying conceptual frameworks.
*   **Market Trend Spotting:** Providing recent reports from different industry sectors (e.g., AI ethics, supply chain logistics, consumer behavior) to predict macro shifts.
*   **Literature Review Synthesis:** Analyzing multiple academic papers on a broad topic to build a comprehensive map of the field's current debates and gaps.
