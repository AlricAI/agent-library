## Overview
The Research Coordinator acts as a master planner for complex research initiatives. Instead of tackling large, ambiguous questions directly, this agent analyzes the scope, breaks it down into interconnected sub-tasks, and designs an optimal workflow that delegates work to specialized agents.

It is designed for users who have ambitious, multi-disciplinary research goals that require coordination across several different domains (e.g., market analysis combined with technical feasibility studies).

## Capabilities
*   **Complexity Analysis:** Assesses the depth and breadth of a given research query to determine necessary components.
*   **Task Decomposition:** Breaks down monolithic problems into discrete, actionable sub-tasks.
*   **Specialist Allocation:** Matches specific tasks to the most appropriate specialized AI agents based on their defined expertise.
*   **Workflow Sequencing:** Builds logical, dependency-aware workflows, defining the order in which research streams must occur.
*   **Iteration Planning:** Designs checkpoints and feedback loops to ensure comprehensive coverage and refinement of findings.
*   **Deliverable Structuring:** Provides a complete plan including timelines, risk assessments, and success metrics.

## Example Use Cases
*   **Market Entry Strategy:** You need to understand the market viability for a new sustainable gadget. The Coordinator will break this into tasks like 'Regulatory Landscape Analysis' (Legal Agent), 'Consumer Adoption Rate Prediction' (Finance Agent), and 'Material Sourcing Feasibility' (Research Agent).
*   **Academic Literature Review:** For a thesis topic, it can map out which literature review components need to be tackled in parallel while ensuring the final synthesis integrates conflicting findings.
*   **Competitive Deep Dive:** When analyzing a competitor, it coordinates tasks across product features, financial health, and public perception into one cohesive strategic report plan.
