# YouAndINotAI User Research Plan

## Overview

This document outlines the user research strategy for YouAndINotAI, focusing on validating our design decisions, improving accessibility, and ensuring our trust-building features effectively support genuine community connection.

## Research Objectives

### Primary Goals

1. Validate effectiveness of safety and trust-building features
2. Assess accessibility compliance with real users
3. Understand user mental models for community engagement
4. Identify pain points in meetup and volunteering discovery
5. Evaluate mobile-first design implementation success

### Secondary Goals

1. Measure user confidence in platform privacy controls
2. Assess discoverability of key features
3. Identify opportunities for feature enhancement
4. Gather feedback on visual design and branding
5. Understand user motivations for real-world community engagement

## Target Participant Profiles

### Core Demographics

- **Age Range**: 18-65 years old
- **Geographic Distribution**: US-based participants with diverse urban/rural representation
- **Technical Comfort**: Mixed levels from beginner to advanced smartphone users
- **Disability Representation**: Include participants with various accessibility needs

### User Segmentation

1. **Community Seekers** (40%) - Actively looking for local connections and events
2. **Volunteer Enthusiasts** (25%) - Interested in volunteering opportunities and giving back
3. **Safety-Conscious Users** (20%) - Prioritize privacy and safety in online interactions
4. **Accessibility Users** (10%) - Depend on assistive technologies for platform use
5. **New Platform Adopters** (5%) - Recently joined social platforms, need onboarding support

### Recruitment Criteria

- Currently seeking genuine community connections (not dating-focused)
- Own a smartphone and regularly use social platforms
- Willing to participate in remote testing sessions
- Represent diverse backgrounds, abilities, and perspectives
- No prior extensive experience with similar platforms (to avoid bias)

## Research Methods

### 1. Usability Testing

**Objective**: Evaluate effectiveness of core feature flows

**Participants**: 15-20 users across all segments
**Sessions**: 45-60 minutes remote moderated sessions
**Focus Areas**:

- Safety feature discovery and usage
- Event and volunteering search/filter workflows
- Privacy control navigation and understanding
- Onboarding and account setup process
- Reporting/blocking functionality

**Metrics**:

- Task success rate (%)
- Time on task (seconds)
- Error rate per task
- System Usability Scale (SUS) score
- Confidence ratings (1-5 scale)

### 2. Accessibility Evaluation

**Objective**: Validate WCAG 2.1 AA compliance with assistive technology users

**Participants**: 8-10 users with disabilities
**Sessions**: 60-90 minutes remote moderated sessions
**Focus Areas**:

- Screen reader navigation and comprehension
- Keyboard-only operation effectiveness
- Color contrast and visual impairment accommodations
- Cognitive accessibility for neurodivergent users
- Motor disability interface interaction

**Metrics**:

- Accessibility compliance checklist completion
- Task completion without assistance (%)
- Frustration indicators during tasks
- Feature accessibility rating (1-5 scale)
- Recommendations for improvement count

### 3. Contextual Inquiry

**Objective**: Observe natural platform usage and environmental factors

**Participants**: 10-12 users in their natural environments
**Sessions**: 90-120 minutes unmoderated observation
**Focus Areas**:

- Real-world usage contexts (commute, home, community centers)
- Interruption handling during platform use
- Multi-tasking behaviors with platform
- Social sharing and discussion about platform
- Platform usage in group/community settings

**Metrics**:

- Observation checklist completion
- Environmental constraint identification
- Social influence factor documentation
- Usage pattern categorization
- Pain point frequency logging

### 4. Surveys and Questionnaires

**Objective**: Gather quantitative data on user attitudes and experiences

**Participants**: 100+ active platform users
**Format**: Online self-administered surveys
**Focus Areas**:

- Trust and safety perceptions
- Community connection satisfaction
- Feature usage frequency
- Platform recommendation likelihood
- Demographic and behavioral segmentation

**Metrics**:

- Net Promoter Score (NPS)
- Trust Index (custom 10-item scale)
- Feature satisfaction ratings (1-5 scale)
- Usage frequency distributions
- Correlation analysis between demographics and satisfaction

### 5. Focus Groups

**Objective**: Explore collective experiences and social dynamics

**Participants**: 4-6 participants per group, 3 groups total
**Sessions**: 90 minutes moderated discussions
**Focus Areas**:

- Community building experiences and expectations
- Safety concerns and trust-building factors
- Volunteering and meetup experiences
- Platform feature priorities and suggestions
- Social norms and group behavior observations

**Metrics**:

- Theme identification and frequency
- Sentiment analysis of qualitative responses
- Feature request categorization
- Concern categorization and severity rating
- Group consensus level on key topics

## Research Timeline

### Phase 1: Preparation (Weeks 1-2)

- Finalize research protocols and materials
- Recruit participants through panel providers and community outreach
- Train moderators and observers
- Set up testing environment and recording systems
- Obtain ethical approval for research activities

### Phase 2: Pilot Testing (Week 3)

- Conduct 2-3 pilot sessions with internal team members
- Refine protocols based on pilot feedback
- Validate testing materials and technical setup
- Adjust recruitment criteria if needed
- Confirm data collection and analysis procedures

### Phase 3: Primary Research (Weeks 4-7)

- Execute usability testing sessions (15-20 participants)
- Conduct accessibility evaluation sessions (8-10 participants)
- Run contextual inquiry observations (10-12 participants)
- Launch survey distribution to 100+ users
- Schedule and conduct focus groups (3 sessions)

### Phase 4: Analysis and Reporting (Weeks 8-9)

- Data transcription and coding for qualitative research
- Statistical analysis of quantitative data
- Identify key insights and prioritized recommendations
- Create detailed findings report with supporting evidence
- Present findings to stakeholders for discussion

### Phase 5: Implementation Planning (Week 10)

- Collaborate with product team on prioritization
- Develop detailed implementation roadmap
- Create specific design iteration plans
- Establish metrics for measuring improvement
- Schedule follow-up validation research

## Methodology Details

### Usability Testing Protocol

#### Pre-Session Questionnaire

- Platform usage frequency and history
- Primary goals for using YouAndINotAI
- Self-rated comfort with technology (1-5 scale)
- Previous experience with similar platforms

#### Testing Scenarios

1. **Safety Feature Discovery**
   - Task: Find and use the blocking feature for a user
   - Task: Locate and adjust privacy settings
   - Task: Report an inappropriate message or profile

2. **Community Feature Navigation**
   - Task: Search for volunteering opportunities by interest
   - Task: Create and publish a community meetup event
   - Task: RSVP to attend a discovered event

3. **Privacy and Data Control**
   - Task: Review and modify data sharing preferences
   - Task: Request a copy of personal data
   - Task: Delete a conversation thread

#### Post-Session Interview

- Overall platform satisfaction rating (1-10)
- Most challenging aspects of platform use
- Trust level in platform safety features
- Suggestions for improvement
- Likelihood to recommend platform to others

### Accessibility Evaluation Framework

#### Screen Reader Testing

- Navigation using VoiceOver (iOS) and TalkBack (Android)
- Content comprehension and structure understanding
- Form field identification and completion
- Error message clarity and guidance
- Shortcut key efficiency and memorability

#### Keyboard Navigation

- Complete primary tasks using keyboard only
- Focus order logicality and predictability
- Visual focus indicator visibility
- Skip link and navigation efficiency
- Modal dialog trap avoidance

#### Visual Impairment Accommodations

- Color blindness simulation testing impact
- Text resizing functionality assessment
- High contrast mode effectiveness
- Iconography comprehension without color
- Low vision reading experience

#### Cognitive Accessibility

- Clear and simple language comprehension
- Consistent navigation and interaction patterns
- Error prevention and recovery clarity
- Instructions and help system effectiveness
- Distraction minimization and focus support

### Survey Instrument Structure

#### Sections

1. **Demographics**
   - Age, gender, location, occupation
   - Technology usage patterns
   - Disability status (optional)

2. **Platform Usage**
   - Frequency and duration of use
   - Primary features utilized
   - Session context and environment

3. **Trust and Safety**
   - Safety feature awareness and usage
   - Comfort level with community interactions
   - Privacy control understanding and satisfaction
   - Incident reporting experience

4. **Community Connection**
   - Real-world meetup participation
   - Volunteering activity facilitated
   - Sense of belonging and inclusion
   - Platform's impact on social connections

5. **User Experience**
   - Overall satisfaction rating
   - Ease of use assessment
   - Feature discovery efficiency
   - Mobile experience satisfaction
   - Technical performance perception

6. **Recommendation and Future**
   - Likelihood to recommend (NPS)
   - Feature enhancement priorities
   - Pricing/model feedback (if applicable)
   - Long-term platform usage intention

## Data Collection and Analysis

### Quantitative Analysis

- Descriptive statistics for all numerical data
- Inferential statistics for group comparisons
- Correlation analysis between variables
- Regression modeling for predictor identification
- Statistical significance testing (p<0.05)

### Qualitative Analysis

- Thematic analysis using open coding methodology
- Constant comparison approach for pattern identification
- Member checking for verification of interpretations
- Triangulation across multiple data sources
- Quotation extraction for illustrative examples

### Mixed-Methods Integration

- Convergence analysis where quantitative and qualitative data align
- Complementarity analysis where methods provide different perspectives
- Elaboration analysis where qualitative data explains quantitative patterns
- Initiation analysis where qualitative findings generate quantitative hypotheses
- Triangulation matrices for cross-validation of findings

## Ethical Considerations

### Informed Consent

- Clear explanation of research purpose and procedures
- Voluntary participation without coercion
- Right to withdraw at any time without penalty
- Confidentiality assurance for all data
- Compensation details and payment arrangements

### Privacy Protection

- Anonymization of all personally identifiable information
- Secure data storage with encryption and access controls
- Limited data retention per institutional guidelines
- No sharing of individual responses without explicit permission
- Compliance with relevant data protection regulations (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)

### Vulnerable Population Considerations

- Additional safeguards for participants with disabilities
- Trauma-informed approach to safety feature discussions
- Cultural sensitivity in recruitment and testing
- Language accessibility for non-native English speakers
- Economic accessibility through varied compensation options

## Stakeholder Communication

- Technical issues and resolution documentation
- Participant recruitment and retention tracking
- Budget and resource allocation monitoring

### Final Presentation

- ROI projections and impact estimation
