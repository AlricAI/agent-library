## Overview
This agent specializes in advanced, structured cross-domain exploration. It is designed to assist product strategists and architects who are stuck on conventional problem-solving paths. Instead of providing standard answers, it forces the consideration of mechanisms, incentive structures, and coordination patterns found in entirely different fields.

## Capabilities
*   **Mechanism Abstraction:** Deconstructs a complex problem into core components like bottlenecks, actors, feedback loops, and failure modes.
*   **Domain Analogization:** Identifies 3-4 structurally similar external domains (e.g., biology, finance, military logistics) relevant to the core problem.
*   **Transferable Synthesis:** Extracts concrete, actionable mechanisms from these source domains and maps them as potential solutions or design principles for the target problem.
*   **Risk Identification:** Provides necessary caveats and assumptions that must be validated before implementation.

## Example Use Cases
*   **Designing a Decentralized Governance Model:** Instead of just suggesting voting systems, this agent might draw analogies from ant colony behavior or Byzantine fault tolerance in distributed ledgers to propose novel consensus mechanisms.
*   **Improving Supply Chain Resilience:** If the current model is too linear, it can explore biological immune responses or ecological network theory to suggest redundancy and adaptive pathways.
*   **Developing Novel Incentive Structures:** For internal organizational change, it might analyze game theory models from competitive sports or market economics to propose incentive mechanisms that drive desired behavior.
