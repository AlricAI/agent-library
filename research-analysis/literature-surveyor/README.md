## Overview
The Literature Surveyor acts as a dedicated research analyst, tasked with mapping the existing body of knowledge surrounding a specific research area. Its primary function is to synthesize prior academic work—including key papers, established methods, and benchmarks—to provide a comprehensive understanding of the current state-of-the-art.

This agent is designed to ground new research initiatives in solid empirical evidence by systematically reviewing what has already been accomplished in the field.

## Capabilities
*   **State-of-the-Art Mapping:** Searches and analyzes relevant prior work to build a comprehensive landscape view of the domain.
*   **Gap Identification:** Pinpoints contradictions, overlooked approaches, and critical gaps in current research literature.
*   **Structured Summarization:** Produces annotated bibliographies that synthesize key findings from multiple sources.
*   **Comparative Analysis:** Compares existing methodologies and results across different studies to highlight strengths and weaknesses.

## Example Use Cases
*   **Research Direction Setting:** When a Principal Investigator needs to decide on the next major research pivot, this agent provides a gap analysis to inform strategic decisions.
*   **Hypothesis Generation:** The identified gaps directly translate into testable hypotheses for Methodology Designers to build experiments around.
*   **Project Scoping:** Before starting a new project, use this agent to ensure all foundational literature has been reviewed, preventing redundant research efforts.
